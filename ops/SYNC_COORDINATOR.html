BlockDAG Sync Coordinator

The coordinator avoids wasting bandwidth and disk IO after a major restore. When backend nodes are far behind, it pauses one follower, lets the best leader catch up alone, then resumes or seeds the follower.

Normal mode

Both nodes run independently and see different peers once they are near tip.

Large catch-up

One healthy leader imports while the follower is stopped to reduce duplicate download and write pressure.

Recovery

When the leader is near tip, the follower can be resumed or safely seeded from the leader with a short final leader stop.

Commands

# Read-only decision
python3 ops/sync_coordinator.py --once

# Allow automatic pause/resume, but not data replacement
python3 ops/sync_coordinator.py --once --repair --pause-follower --resume-follower

# Seed the paused follower from the leader with a consistent final copy
python3 ops/sync_coordinator.py --once --repair --pause-follower --seed-follower --resume-follower --allow-leader-stop

Safety Rules

RuleReason
Never seed from a leader unless it is near tip.Copying stale leader data just moves the catch-up delay to the other node.
Follower pause is only used during large catch-up.Near tip, dual independent nodes are better for uptime and peer diversity.
Safe seeding requires --allow-leader-stop.LevelDB chain data should have a stopped final rsync before it becomes the follower's database.
